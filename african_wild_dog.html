African Wild Dog

The african wild dog, lycaon pictus, part of the canidae family.
They are very endangered and has a population of 5,000 dogs roaming.
African wild dogs have a lifespan of 10 years in total. They are found in the savanna of Saharan Africa.
They use to teamwork to take down large prey, superb hearing and sense of smell.
Africa wild dogs are carnivorous and they feed on antelopes, warthogs, wildebeest, and etc.
